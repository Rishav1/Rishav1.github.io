I work on **differential privacy** — the mathematics of using people's data without exposing the people. I'm currently the **Head of Product** at [Betterdata](https://www.betterdata.ai/), a Singapore deep-tech startup building privacy-preserving synthetic data, where I earlier joined as a privacy engineer and wrote the privacy-auditing engine at the heart of its platform.

I recently completed my **Ph.D. at the [NUS School of Computing](https://comp.nus.edu.sg)**, advised by [Prof. Xiaokui Xiao](https://www.comp.nus.edu.sg/~xiaoxk/). My research spans **private optimization, privacy auditing, and machine unlearning**. Along the way I proved the first $$O(1)$$ privacy bound for Noisy Gradient Descent (a NeurIPS 2021 spotlight), and my team ran the first independent audit of Apple's `DifferentialPrivacy.framework` — the code privatising analytics on billions of devices — which won a **Distinguished Paper Award at [IEEE S&P 2026](https://sp2026.ieee-security.org/)**.

Before Singapore, I earned my B.Tech in Computer Science from [IIT Guwahati](https://www.iitg.ac.in/cse/), spent a summer as a student researcher at **Google Brain**, and held a research fellowship at the **Max Planck Institute for Software Systems**.
